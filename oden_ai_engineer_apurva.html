How I’d approach the AI Engineer role at Oden

1. Understand factories, not just dataframes
  • Start by understanding lines, processes, and constraints from operators and process engineers: where variability hurts, what “good” looks like, and which levers are safe to adjust.
  • Map those insights onto available data sources (telemetry, logs, ERP/MES context) to see what’s realistically modelable.
2. Design agents & models around concrete objectives
  • Translate business goals (e.g., reduce scrap rate by X%, stabilize cycle time) into modeling and agent objectives with clear evaluation metrics.
  • Prototype agents that observe → plan → act → learn using a mix of ML models and LLM‑based reasoning where appropriate.
3. Build, evaluate, and harden for production
  • Implement agents and models in clean, testable Python, integrating with Oden’s data and API layers.
  • Define an evaluation plan: offline backtests + online pilots, monitoring latency, stability, and business impact.
4. Iterate with customers in the loop
  • Work directly with customers and internal teams to inspect behavior, gather feedback, and refine agent policies.
  • Document how systems behave and why, making it easier to trust, debug, and extend them.
